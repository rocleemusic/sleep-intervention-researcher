## Field notes

| Field | What it captures | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Crash time | When the body went offline involuntarily | Primary indicator of sleep pressure and circadian phase |
| Pre-bed sleep | Any sleep before getting into bed | Often the core variable — 3 hours on the couch is not a nap |
| Bed time | When you got into bed | Defines the crash-to-bed window |
| Onset | Time from bed to sleep | Captures sleep initiation; separates onset from total |
| Mid-night wakes | Wake-ups after initial sleep | Distinguishes continuity problems from total duration problems |
| Wake time | When sleep ended | Determines total possible sleep window |
| Alarm | Whether sleep was cut short | Critical for interpreting total — alarmed nights are not free data |
| Total sleep | Actual duration (not time in bed) | The outcome variable everything else is trying to explain |
| Feeling (1–5) | Subjective quality on a consistent scale | Catches what total misses — 6h interrupted ≠ 6h solid |
| Work end time | When the workday ended | Sets the waking window; hours awake before crash |
| Dinner time | Last substantial meal | Tests the postprandial hypothesis against adenosine timing |
| Caffeine cutoff | Last caffeine of the day | Half-life 5–7 hours — afternoon caffeine is active at midnight |
| Active interventions | What changed this night | Allows correlation across nights; required to evaluate any intervention |

---

## Minimum for investigation

**5 nights** with at least the ✱ fields before any pattern investigation begins. Fewer nights is orientation only — not investigation.

**Before adding any intervention:** Log 7+ nights at baseline first, then 7+ with the single change. Simultaneous changes cannot be evaluated separately.
